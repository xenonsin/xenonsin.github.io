Red
About the project

From May 15th to 25th 2014, I participated in the Public Domain Jam, where we had to make a game based on content found in the public domain.

Red is an Action RPG / Grandma Defence where you play as little red riding hood trying to protect her grandma and herself through waves of wolves.

In terms of combat, I drew inspiration from games such as Bastion, where the keyboard is used to move while the mouse is used for aiming and fighting. I'm not a big fan of the mouse-centric bundling of both movement and combat found in games such as Diablo. I wanted to design a game where combat felt responsive and engaging, and I feel like separating movement and combat to the mouse and keyboard achieves that.

With design and aesthetic, I'm a big fan of Ragnarok Online and it's 2.5D style with 2D sprites in a 3D environtment. I created sprite sheets for both the player and the wolves, while making the 3D trees in blender. I made this effect more apparent by allowing the player to rotate the camera, while updating the sprites to "face" the same direction.

Here's what I was able to accomplish:

  • • Implemented a 3D aware sprite system, which automatically updates the sprites depending on the direction the object is facing in 3D space and where the camera is oriented.
  • • Implemented A* Pathfinding and an appropriate enemy AI for the wolves to move through the level and decide when and who to attack.
  • • Created GUI which updates depending on change in hp events being sent out by the player and grandma.
  • • Used object pooling to store the wolves to avoid slow down when instantiating at the start of each wave.
  • • Created all the art assets myself.
  • • Used an online virtual cello to create the background music.

itch.io jam page

The game was made using Unity and written in C#. 2D art assets were created using CLIP STUDIO PAINT, while 3D assets were created using Blender. Sounds were edited using Audacity and recorded from here.
 



 
Controls:
WASD - Move
Mouse1 or K - Attack
Space - Dash
Shift + Mouse2 - Rotate Camera
Scroll - Zoom In/Out
